This is the complete soft top guide for every Scrambler year.

The Jeep Scrambler CJ-8 — All Years Overview

The Jeep Scrambler CJ-8 was Jeep's factory pickup truck based on the CJ-7 platform, produced 1981–1985 in limited numbers. Key specs that affect top fitment:

  • 1981: First year, AMC 258 I-6 standard. Some first-year variations in weather stripping.
  • 1982–1983: Most common production years. Good parts availability for this range.
  • 1984: Introduction of the Governor Pacer edition. Same top fitment as standard CJ-8.
  • 1985: Final production year. Same top fitment as 1982–1984.

Windshield dimensions and soft top mounting points are consistent across all CJ-8 production years. The truck bed section behind the cab is open — factory "tops" covered the cab area only, not the bed.

Factory vs Replacement vs Restoration — Three Top Strategies

CJ-8 owners face three distinct soft top strategies depending on their goals:

Factory-Style Restoration

For show vehicles and concours-correct Scramblers, using period-correct top materials and hardware matters. Factory CJ-8 tops used a vinyl-coated canvas material with specific snap patterns. Sources for factory-correct materials are limited:

  • Bestop's vintage reproduction line covers some CJ-8 styles
  • Specialty suppliers like Spice/Denim fabric sources for the color-matched fabric
  • Custom canvas shops that work from original pattern drawings

Modern Replacement

Most CJ-8 owners choose modern fabric replacement tops. These use superior materials but look factory-correct in profile and snap pattern. Bestop's Supertop is the dominant choice in this category.

Full Fabric Replacement

For working Scramblers that see regular use, a durable modern top with updated hardware (modern snap connectors vs. original bail-style hardware) is the practical choice. Better weather sealing, longer lifespan, and easier on/off.

Weather Condition Guide — Matching the Top to Your Climate

The Scrambler's vintage design creates unique weather challenges. No modern insulation, limited factory weather stripping, older body-panel gaps. Top selection should account for your primary climate:

  • Dry/desert climates (Southwest): UV resistance is the primary concern. Bestop's Stratablend II resists UV far better than standard vinyl. Avoid solid black tops in desert sun — they absorb significantly more heat.
  • Wet climates (Pacific Northwest, Southeast): Seam sealing and window clarity matter most. Soft top window inserts on older Scramblers are a common leak point. A top with integrated zippered windows (not snapped) seals better.
  • Cold climates: Vinyl tops become stiff and brittle at sustained below-freezing temps. Cloth tops with vinyl backing handle cold better. Avoid forcing windows or zippers when the material is cold.
  • All-weather / four seasons: A full hard top is technically superior for all-weather use. For soft-top-only Scramblers, Bestop's Supertop offers the best all-climate performance of soft options available.

CJ-8 Soft Top Frame — Inspection, Repair, and Replacement

Before purchasing a new soft top, inspect your CJ-8's frame thoroughly. New fabric on a bent or rusted frame won't seal properly and will look worse than a well-maintained older top on a good frame.

Frame Inspection Checklist

  • Bow tubes: Each bow should be straight — hold it up and sight down the tube. Bent bows prevent the top from reaching the sides evenly.
  • Pivot joints: Each pivot should move freely through its range. Seized pivots prevent folding and will stress new fabric if forced. Penetrating oil and gradual working usually frees them; corrosion-welded pivots require replacement.
  • Mounting brackets: Inspect where the frame attaches to the Jeep body. Surface rust is normal; structural rust (pitting, loss of material) in these brackets requires repair or replacement before new fabric.
  • Threading on nut plates: The body's nut plates that accept the frame mounting bolts are a known failure point on high-mileage Scramblers. If threads strip, a helicoil repair or replacement nut plate is required before re-mounting.

Sourcing Frame Components

Individual CJ-8 frame components are becoming increasingly difficult to source as the vehicle ages. Best current sources:

  • Bestop: Carries replacement bow sets for their specific top patterns. Contact Bestop directly with your top's part number for correct bow specifications.
  • Quadratec vintage section: Periodically stocks NOS bow and hardware components for CJ-era Jeeps.
  • CJ-8 community Facebook groups: Parts vehicles being parted out often yield frame components in better condition than what's on running vehicles.
  • Custom fabrication: For severely corroded frames, a local metal fabricator can replicate simple bow sections from DOM steel tubing to original dimensions.

Weather Strip Replacement — The Foundation of a Weathertight Top

New fabric on top of degraded weather stripping leaks. The windshield header seal and door frame seals are wear items that must be addressed as part of any top replacement on a 40-year-old vehicle:

  • Windshield header seal: The rubber strip that runs along the top of the windshield frame. Original seals are universally hardened and cracked on Scramblers. Bestop and Quadratec both stock reproduction header seals.
  • Door frame seals: The channel seals along the door opening that the soft top body flap seals against. Degraded door seals allow water infiltration regardless of top quality.
  • Tailgate weatherstrip: The seal between the cab section and the truck bed area. Often overlooked, but a significant water infiltration point in wet climates.

Frequently Asked Questions — Jeep Scrambler Tops

What years did Jeep make the Scrambler CJ-8?

The Jeep Scrambler CJ-8 was produced from 1981 through 1985. Total production was approximately 27,000 units across all years. 1982 and 1983 were the highest production years.

Does a Jeep CJ-7 soft top fit the CJ-8 Scrambler?

The CJ-7 and CJ-8 share the same cab dimensions and top mounting points forward of the tailgate. A CJ-7 full soft top fits the CJ-8 cab section. However, the Scrambler's truck bed section behind the cab is unique to the CJ-8 configuration.

Where can I find replacement snap hardware for a vintage Scrambler top?

Bestop, Quadratec, and Morris 4x4 Center all stock replacement snap hardware for CJ-8 tops. Bestop specifically lists hardware kits for their vintage Scrambler top patterns. Marine canvas suppliers are a secondary source for generic snaps that match original specifications.

What's the most common failure point on a Scrambler soft top?

The rear side windows are the most common failure point — the original transparent vinyl yellows, crazes, and ultimately cracks. These windows can be replaced independently without replacing the full top. Specialty suppliers sell cut-to-fit replacement transparent vinyl.

Are Scrambler hard tops available?

Yes — original factory hard tops occasionally appear on the used market but command significant premiums ($2,000–$8,000 in good condition). Reproduction fiberglass and ABS hard tops are available from specialty Jeep suppliers but are expensive ($1,500+) and less common than soft top options.

How do I prevent my CJ-8 soft top from mildewing?

Mildew on Scrambler tops results from storing a damp top folded. Always dry the top completely before folding — prop the top open in dry weather after a wet drive. Treat canvas-based tops with a fabric protectant spray (303 Aerospace Protectant is widely recommended) annually.

Can I put a modern JL Wrangler top on a Scrambler?

No — modern Wrangler tops are designed for completely different mounting systems, body dimensions, and windshield frame geometries. CJ-8 tops must be CJ-8 specific or CJ-7 compatible (which share the same cab architecture).
